# Code of conduct

## The short version

Be decent. Help each other. Don't be the person who makes others dread opening their notifications.

## The longer version

This project follows the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). Here's what that means in practice:

**Welcome here:** People of all backgrounds, experience levels, and perspectives. Whether you're filing your first issue or your hundredth PR, you belong.

**Not welcome here:** Harassment, trolling, personal attacks, publishing others' private information, or any behavior that makes someone feel unsafe or unwelcome. This includes "well, actually" gatekeeping disguised as technical feedback.

**Constructive disagreement is fine.** "I think this approach has problems because X" is useful. "This is stupid" is not. Argue the idea, not the person.

## Scope

This applies everywhere the project lives: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any space where you're representing or interacting with stitch-kit.

## Enforcement

If someone is making the space worse, reach out to **gabi@booplex.com**. Reports are handled privately. Responses depend on severity, from a conversation to a permanent ban. The goal is always to fix the situation, not to punish.
